Meet Tokyo’s New Leader!

Japan’s capital city elects its first female governor.

It’s been a big year for women. Rome got its first female mayor, Virginia Raggi. And Theresa May became the second woman to lead Britain. On Sunday, another woman got a win. Tokyo elected its first female governor!

Yuriko Koike is the new leader. Koike studied in Egypt. She speaks Arabic! She worked as a translator and a TV news anchor. Then she went into politics. In 1992, she started working in the government.

Koike was Japan’s environment minister. She started a program called “cool biz.” It lets workers wear cooler clothing. That means no ties or jackets! That way, businesses use less energy on air conditioning.

Hiroya Masuda and Shuntaro Torigoe also ran for governor. (See Slide Show.) But Koike won by more than a million votes! Tokyo’s former governor was Yoichi Masuzoe. He stepped down in June. He was part of a money scandal. Masuzoe used government money to buy vacations.

Koike has a big job to do. Tokyo has more than 13 million people! And the city has a huge event to get ready for — the 2020 Olympics! Koike says she will get the city ready to host the Games.
