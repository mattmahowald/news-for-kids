“A Big, Stinky Flower!”

New Yorkers line up for a whiff of a corpse flower bloom!

“It smells like really bad garbage,” said Natalia, age 11.

“It’s like a rotten egg,” said 9-year-old Kofi.

Luis, age 10, also described the corpse flower. “It’s like a skunk and trash,” he told News-O-Matic. “Mixed together.”

These kids lined up to see — and smell — the famous corpse flower. The huge flower blooms about every 10 years. When it does, the bloom only lasts for 24–36 hours. That makes it a very rare event!

On Thursday, a corpse flower bloomed at the New York Botanical Garden (NYBG). Thousands of people waited for a whiff. And, boy, did it stink!

The flower is named for a dead body — because that’s what it smells like! It’s like a rotting animal on a hot day. Ew! The awful smell attracts bugs for pollination.

Then there’s its size. The flower can grow to 10 feet (3 m). That’s taller than any person! Between its stinky smell and huge height, the corpse flower was quite a show. That was great news for NYBG director Marc Hachadourian.

“This is nine years of hard work,” said Hachadourian. The flower comes from the rainforest of Sumatra. But he helped cultivate it in the Bronx, New York. “It’s a real thrill.” He looked at the huge crowd. “It’s a big, stinky flower,” he said. “They all want to smell it!”

But why would anyone want to smell this flower? It smells “like the back of a restaurant,” according to 10-year-old Joanna. Just ask her 12-year-old brother, Erik. (They’re on the front page.)

“This is a once-in-a-lifetime opportunity,” said Erik. “It doesn’t happen every day.”

Maybe you will be lucky — or unlucky — enough to smell one someday.
